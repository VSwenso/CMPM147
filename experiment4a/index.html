Experiment 4a - Infinite Worlds

Description

This was the first of three generators that I created with my partner for this assignment, Tory. This is a world where an infinite amount of concentric rings around the origin of the world are updated over time to have a steady mix of colors. Each ring has its own color which is based on the world's seed.

Technical

To create this world, colors were assigned to each tile based on their distance from the origin of the world. It is a tweaked version of Manhattan distance to show a ring like effect. Then, over time, the RGB values for each ring are modified by a scale dependent on the world seed to give it a life-like effect.

Reflection

We are very happy with the outcome of this world. On certain seeds, the color updates in a way that it looks like the rings of colors are converging towards the center. In other seeds, the opposite happens, where it looks like the colors are emminating from the origin. This world satisfies all the requirements of the assignment. The only way we could have made it more interesting was to displace the tiles based on their color, but when we tried that it just made ugly gray holes in the world, so we opted out of that.
